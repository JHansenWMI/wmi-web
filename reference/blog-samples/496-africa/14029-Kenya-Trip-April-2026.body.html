Kenya Trip April 2026

6/11/2026
Pastoral Articles, Mission Trips, Africa

April 1, 2026, my family and I arrived in Nairobi, Kenya at 9:55 pm. Bishop Geoffrey Njuguna graciously picked us up at the airport and took us to his home, where we stayed for three nights before moving into Nairobi.

This 27-day mission trip was both important and busy. Each day was dedicated to various forms of ministry, ranging from conferences to church services and individual meetings. We focused on extending the Kingdom of God and discussing ways to continue expanding God’s Kingdom.

On April 2, I had an opportunity to pray for Rev. Dr. Judy Mbugua at her home, after which we visited and prayed for Pastor Elizabeth Njuguna’s mother. The following day, April 3, Bishop Njuguna and I spoke at Deliverance Ignite International Conference, and I later held a private meeting with a leader that evening.

On April 4th, in addition to meeting with Bishop Geoffrey Njuguna in the morning, I met with Bishop Mike Adipo and later with Apostle Joe Mwaniki. Each of these meetings dealt with Kenya and the nations, and what the church leaders must do to bring salvation and transformation rather than tyranny and judgement.

On Sunday, April 5, my wife Dr. Adalia and I had the privilege of ministering at Langata Deliverance Church in Nairobi, pastored by Bishop Geoffrey Njuguna. The service was notably attended by the former Vice President of Kenya and 2027 Kenyan Presidential candidate Stephen Kalonzo Musuyoka (SKM), along with approximately 50 members of parliament. During my sermon I focused on Resurrection Day and the question, “What DNA are you?” My wife also actively participated by praying for many individuals and a powerful moment occurred when a lady was delivered from a demon at the feet of Hon. Musyoka. The service was impactful as I called for a national repentance to save Kenya from tyranny and to ensure the integrity of the elections.

On April 6, we met with Prophet Paul and Prophetess Janet Songo to discuss the current situation in Kenya and to strategize on the necessary steps to bring about salvation for the nation.

On the following day, April 7th, I spoke at the gathering of the Kenya Coalition of Church Alliances & Ministers. The top church leaders, including archbishops, were in attendance. I spoke on the need for National Repentance and highlighted how the New World Order (NWO) Elite have chosen their leaders in the nations and emphasized that only the church can protect the integrity of elections. The elite invest heavily in candidates so they become puppets, forcing the people to become slaves and take a mark/chip so they cannot buy or sell without it. Christians must ensure that men and women of Christian morality are elected to leadership so that nations remain under God’s principles and not the NWO and tyranny.

That evening, I met with Bishop Dr. Constantine Mwadime and the Hon. Dennis Mutuku, to discuss the importance of National Repentance and practical economic solutions that can help both people and nations.

On April 8th, we met with Bishop Eliphas Mitugo and that evening had dinner with SKM at his residence with the Hon Stephen Mule, MP. We discussed the need for National Repentance among other things.

From April 9-11, I met with various leaders, continually discussing the situation in Kenya and the need for National Repentance.

On Sunday, April 12, we ministered at Bishop Thomas Muthee’s church, Word of Faith, in Kiambu, Kenya. Rev. Phyllis Muthee MC’d the services while Bishop Thomas was away, dedicating a newly built church. In the first service, I spoke on “I am a Debtor,” and in the second service, I ministered on “Moses Rod & Eagles Arise.”

From Monday through Friday, April 13th—17th, Bishop Muthee’s Church hosted the Rebecca Arise Women’s International Conference, where my wife was one of the speakers. Bishop Muthee and I took part in both the opening Monday service and closing Friday service. What a tremendous conference!

Sunday, April 19, we ministered at Tabernacle of Grace City of Hope Church; pastored by Prophet Paul and Prophetess Janet Songo. It was a powerful meeting marked by a strong manifestation of the Holy Spirit.

On April 20th, we continued meeting with leaders. Tuesday the 21st , I met with Bishop Paul Karanja, followed by meetings with Bishop Dennis Nthumbi and that evening with Apostle Joe Mwaniki. My wife met with Christina Shusho a very well-known gospel singer. They discussed helping the poor in Tanzania while I continued to talk to the leaders about National Repentance in the nations and the responsibilities of the Church Leaders.

On Wednesday April 22nd, I recorded three television programs at Prayer TV Kiambu, Kenya. Later in the day, I met with Pastor Joshua and Esther Muthee, followed by Hon. Mutuku.

On Thursday, the 23rd, in addition to breakfast and dinner with leaders, we ministered for Rev. Dr. Judy Mbugua at Homecare Spiritual Fellowship. Dr. Judy is doing a tremendous work among women and children in the slums. Bishop Michael Wafula and Hon. Mutuku also attended this meeting. Besides a summary of the need for National Repentance, I spoke on “Don’t Lose Hope”. The Holy Spirit specifically tailored this message to minister to the women present.

On Friday, the 24th, we again held meetings throughout the day. In between, we met with the Malawi High Commissioner, discussing a return trip to Malawi to meet with the government officials and the initiative to help meet the needs of the poor.

April 25th, I conducted a staff meeting and led a devotional at the WMI Bible College. Later, Bishop Dr. Tobias Nyamwaya, WMI Bible College Director, took Dr. Adalia and me out for lunch.

On Sunday, April 26, we ministered at Kitengela Deliverance Church under Bishop Mohammed Kamwaro. Once again, we had a tremendous service. The remainder of the day was spent meeting with various leaders.

On my final day in Kenya, April 27th, before flying out close to midnight, I continued meeting with different leaders, including another with SKM, the 2027 Kenya Presidential Candidate.

This was a highly productive and impactful mission trip. We believe it is important to return this year to continue the work of pushing for National Repentance and strengthening relationships with church and national leaders.

Please prayerfully consider helping us return through your generous mission love offering. Thank you for your prayers, support, and partnership in advancing the Kingdom of God.
