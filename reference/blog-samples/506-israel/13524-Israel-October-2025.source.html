Israel October 2025

11/26/2025
Pastoral Articles, Mission Trips, Middle East, Israel

On Friday, October 17, 2025, I had the honor of speaking at the Global International 2025 Congress of Apostolic Leaders held in Baltimore, Maryland. 

On Saturday, October 18th, we departed from Baltimore and traveled through several connecting flights; Baltimore to Detroit, then to Seattle, and onward to JFK International Airport in New York, before finally boarding our flight to Tel Aviv, Israel. Due to delays in New York, we experienced an extended layover of approximately 15½ hours before departure.

We arrived at Ben Gurion Airport on Monday, October 20th, around 4:00 p.m., and after three full days of travel, we reached our destination exhausted but thankful. That evening, we checked into the New Imperial Hotel in Old Jerusalem at approximately 7:00 p.m.

This trip to Israel was of great significance. We met daily with various leaders, beginning Tuesday, October 21st, at the Knesset Christian Allies Caucus in the Israeli Parliament, where we participated in discussions addressing global peace initiatives and President Trump’s twenty-point peace plan.

On Wednesday, October 22nd, we had a profound encounter in Old Jerusalem, praying with a Muslim gentleman before visiting and praying at the Wailing Wall (Western Wall). Later that evening, we met with former MK Rabbi Yehuda Glick and his wife, Hadas Dissen Glick, who lead the Amitsim Ministry for widows and orphans.

My wife, Dr. Adalia Hansen, who directs the WMI Dorcas Fund for Widows and Orphans, was deeply moved by their work and expressed her joy in seeing their shared mission to support vulnerable women and children. The WMI Dorcas Fund is now partnering with Amitsim to strengthen and expand these life-changing efforts.

On Thursday, October 23rd, we met with former Israeli Ambassador to Kenya, Michael Lotem, engaging in meaningful dialogue about strengthening faith-based partnerships and regional understanding.

Friday, October 24th, was spent at the Dead Sea, where we filmed television segments for our upcoming ministry broadcast. It was also a joyful moment for our family as our son had the memorable experience of riding a camel near Jericho.

On Saturday morning, October 25th, we visited Golgotha and the Garden Tomb, reflecting deeply on the power of the resurrection. In the afternoon, we traveled to Bethlehem to visit and pray with another Muslim family at the site traditionally recognized as the birthplace of Jesus.

Throughout our stay, we recorded daily television programs on location, capturing the spirit of the Holy Land and the significance of unity among believers. Dr. Adalia Hansen also conducted an insightful interview with former MK Rabbi Yehuda Glick, discussing interfaith collaboration and shared humanitarian efforts. 

On Sunday, October 26th, we departed from Tel Aviv to JFK International Airport, enduring a 13-hour layover before continuing our journey home on Monday morning, October 27th, for Seattle, Washington.

It was indeed a long and tiring journey, but we count it all joy to make the sacrifice in service to our Messiah.
